Ship's presentation

The Motor Vessel “MV CHANG HANG BIN HAI” is a motor-powered single deck Bulk Carrier, all of steel welded construction. The vessel was delivered in the year 2011.She has a raised forecastle with navigation, accommodation and machinery all situated at the afterpart of the vessel. The hatch covers are of Mc Gregor folding type. The vessel is equipped with 5 (five) cargo holds.She is classed by CCS. The vessel was departed from Djen Djen, Algeria on 10th August 2023.She was redelivered at Casablanca, Morocco, by the Charterer CHINALAND SHIPPING PTE LTD, to the Owner SHANGHAI MING WAH SHIPPING CO.LTD on 15th August 2023 at 15:48 hours (LMT) and GMT at 14:48 hours on15th August 2023.

Viste the collectivity

MainDeck

We had carried out a visual inspection of the vessel hull, the main deck & the poop deck, the hatches, the cargo holds, the vessel’s deck, the mooring winches, the anchor windlass, the engine room and the navigation bridge.All of the above, Normal ‘wear & tear’, rust / scale; scratches / abrasions are included in this report. The survey was carried out together with ship’s personnel, the Chief Officer and the Ship’s Crew. The fore castle was found to be in fair condition without any sign of major damages. High surface rusts pot and stains with high frequency pitting were noted on the main deck. The bollards, fair leads and other mooring equipment are found to be in fairly working condition. Anchor windlass with tow checkless anchor sighted & reported in working condition. Hand railing, open railing stanchion generally were fair condition, high surface rust spots and stains but in good condition. The Port/Starboard main deck was not satisfactorily painted and need maintenance. Access ladders, air pipes, sounding pipes and other deck fittings were found intact and in apparent sound condition. Port and starboard passage was well maintained and in a satisfactory condition. Guard rails on port and starboard main deck were noted to be in satisfactory condition with some bends and dents throughout.

External Hull

At time of our attendance, the vessel was moored starboard side alongside with the wharf N° 13 SOMAPORT TERMINAL, Casablanca Port, Casablanca, Morocco. Generally, the condition of her PORT and STARBOARD external hull plating and coating was found to be in fair condition with painted observed along the hull plating. Bulbous bow noted to be well painted. The hull above water level was found to be in fair condition. The draught marks on the bow and stern were fair visible with painted and the Plimsoll mark at starboard side, port side mid-ship of the vessel was fair visible with painted.

Viste the collectivity

Hatch covers & Cargo holds

The hatch coamings and stays were noted to be painted and in working condition with no sign of deformities. The securing cleats on all hatch covers perimeter were noted to be in working and in serviceable condition but some needs to be changed. The vessel is fitted with FIVE (5) cargo holds with hatch covers of MacGregor FOLDING TYPE. Sealing between the hatch cover and coaming is achieved by means of securing cleats, which is fitted to the panel and tightens against a compression bar on top of the coaming. These hatch covers were opened and closed in our presence. All pipes and pipe guards were noted in satisfactory sound condition. The external and internal condition of all hatch covers was noted in apparent sound condition and in good working order.

Wheel House

The condition of the wheelhouse was noted to be clean and tidy. The deck flooring and fully panelled bulkheads including deck head were noted to be in apparent sound condition.

Toughened bridge portholes and weather tight doors were noted in order and in sound condition. All the navigational equipment and communication equipment in the wheelhouse were reported to be in working and serviceable condition. Publications and manuals were reported to have been updated accordingly.The bridge deck plating was well coated; signal with its complement of lights, radar scanner; signal halyards and antennae were found in order.
